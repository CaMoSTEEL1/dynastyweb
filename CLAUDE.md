# DynastyWire

AI-powered media universe companion for College Football 26 Dynasty Mode. Generates full news cycles (articles, press conferences, social reactions, broadcast shows, recruiting storylines) after every game.

## Tech Stack
- Next.js 16 / React 19 / TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS 4 with CSS custom properties
- Supabase (auth, database)
- Anthropic Claude API (content generation)
- Framer Motion (animations)

## Design Context

### Users
College Football 26 Dynasty Mode players who want their game results to feel like more than numbers on a screen. They're deeply invested in their program — tracking records, obsessing over recruits, living and dying with every game. They use DynastyWire between gaming sessions to relive their latest game through AI-generated media: articles, press conferences, social reactions, broadcast shows, and recruiting updates. The job to be done: make every dynasty feel like a real program with a real media universe covering it.

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Authentic. Electric.**

DynastyWire lives at the intersection of raw sports journalism and peak College GameDay energy. It's a late-night ESPN segment crossed with the intensity of a rivalry game broadcast — real talk, no filter, everything dialed to 11. The voice is sharp and unapologetic. Headlines hit hard. Social reactions feel visceral. Press conferences have genuine tension. Nothing about it should feel generated or sterile.

### Emotional Goals
- **Immersion & Authenticity**: Every piece of content should feel like it came from a real sports media ecosystem — beat reporters, Twitter trolls, TV analysts, recruiting insiders
- **Hype & Excitement**: Wins should feel massive, momentum should build, and the user should feel the electricity of a program on the rise
- **Pride & Ownership**: This is YOUR program, YOUR legacy — the media universe reinforces that you're building something real
- **Drama & Tension**: Hot seat pressure, recruiting battles, rival trolling, fan frustration — the emotional rollercoaster that makes CFB compelling

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual Tone:** Dark, editorial, textured — inspired by vintage sports journalism with modern production value. The UI should feel like a premium sports publication, not a web app.

**Theme:** Always dark. Warm brown-black backgrounds (`#1c1a17`) with off-white editorial text. Strategic use of deep crimson (`#b5202a`) for energy and burnt orange (`#d4943a`) for warmth.

**Typography:** Headline serif (Playfair Display) for bold, commanding headlines. Editorial serif (Source Serif 4) for readable body text. Clean sans (Geist) for UI labels and metadata. Heavy use of uppercase tracking on labels and section headers.

**Signature Elements:** Rule diamonds as editorial dividers, drop caps on lead stories, scrolling breaking ticker in crimson, column rules between sections, skeleton pulses for loading states.

**References:** The Athletic's editorial polish, ESPN College GameDay's intensity, vintage newspaper front pages, prestige sports documentary aesthetics.

**Anti-References:**
- NO generic SaaS dashboards — no flat corporate cards, no bland charts-and-metrics energy
- NO cluttered news sites — no ESPN.com ad chaos, no overwhelming sidebars and popups
- Keep personality and texture — never strip down to sterile minimalism

### Design Principles

1. **Editorial First**: Every screen should feel like a page from a premium sports publication. Typography hierarchy, whitespace, and content structure take priority over flashy UI patterns.

2. **Authenticity Over Polish**: Content should feel raw and real — like actual sports media, not AI-generated filler. Rough edges and personality are features, not bugs.

3. **Energy Through Restraint**: The dark palette and muted backgrounds exist so that crimson accents, bold headlines, and key moments HIT. Don't dilute impact by overusing color or animation.

4. **Immersion Is the Product**: Every interaction — from the breaking ticker to the press conference podium to the social feed — should reinforce the fantasy that this is a real media universe covering a real program.

5. **Drama Serves the Story**: Hot seat warnings, fan sentiment, rival trolling, playoff projections — lean into the emotional stakes of college football. The UI should amplify drama, not flatten it.
